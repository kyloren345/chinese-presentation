Buddhist Monasticism in China

The monasticism of Buddhism in China plays probably the most vital role in this culture. This means that either a male or female person Dharmaguptaka Viyana, it has 250 rules for monks and 348 for nuns. The practises and services that the nuns and monks perform are, burning offers in altars, liturgical services, moving around the Buddha hall, preaching the scriptures and performing the Dharma lectures. Of course this list has more to offer but it would take a while to mention everything here. In the history of this culture there have been many monasteries like city monasteries and ones deep in the mountain ranges some of them are even quite large like typical Chinese dynasty monasteries.

Major Traditions in Chinese Buddhism

Most of the Major traditions included focusing on the Lotus sutra and other sutras depending on the school. The way teachings work is that like mentioned previously depending on the school they would focus on one specific sutra in the teachings of the main sutras that were made in that time. This also inspired in some way for the base foundations of Confucianism and Daoism/Taoism, this is because most people thought that the teachings from Buddhism were quite important. This is the turning point where basically Buddhism became the biggest culture in China ruling over 13% of the population. Some of the other traditions are typical Buddhist traditions like visiting temples and the monks/nuns, to even just praying, in my tradition of Buddhism for example we have this thing called a Pirith and basically you recite what it is and it's almost like a prayer but it's more like a blessing type of thing and Chinese Buddhism has a similar thing.
